About us

Our Story

The Nuba Mountains All the people of the Nuba Mountains have known is war. The time to rebuild is now. “Without education we are nothing” Patrick Alalo “History tells us that the Nuba Mountains has experienced prolonged periods of economic and political marginalization, racial discrimination, cultural suppression, conflict and displacement. External pressures such as land seizures and slave raids have pushed the Nuba further into the mountains and off of their agricultural lands.”


A brief chronology of events in the Nuba Mountains that necessitated the need for this education initiative;

  • January 1, 1956: Sudan gained its independence.

  • 1955–1972: First Sudanese Civil War.

  • September 1983: A second war killing over 2 million people erupts between North and South Sudan.

  • February 11, 1985: Led by Yusif Kuwa the Nuba Join the Liberation struggle.

  • April 6, 1985: Then President Nimeiry is overthrown.

  • April 1986: A new government forms in Sudan

What We Do

  • Work with refugee parents to assist them understand the Australian education system and to ensure their kids understand the benefits of attending school.

  • Work with refugee students to provide tutoring in groups with objectives that are aligned to the student’s individual need.

  • Partner with existing educational facilities in the community, we assist youth to create higher education pathways and to work towards completing university.

  • FEducate parents on how to improve interactions with their kids at home.

  • Create resources in our community centers like computer labs and libraries, available to local students.

  • Work with schools and other community groups to provide value added training such as computer literacy, arts and higher education pathways planning

The Nuba mountains

The People of the Nuba Mountains experience pervasive poverty, War trauma, lack basic health care services and poor nutrition. Education is the only way out.

In a region where every family has lived the experience of the war, many children are either orphaned or supported by single parents most of whom are mothers. Children especially young girls have been forced to remain at home to take care of their family, walking for days on end in search of water and food to eat the family, they are also tasked with general household duties which make it difficult to attain an education.

For every girl educated, 7 more people benefit. The aim of One in Four is to promote equal opportunity and access to quality education for all children of the Nuba Mountains and Africa as a continent. We aim to build thriving, sustainable and self reliant communities wherever we work through Education initiatives that promote attainment of knowledge for all who seek.
